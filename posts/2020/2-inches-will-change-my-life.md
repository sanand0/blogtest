<h3><strong>I walked ~11 million steps in the last 3 years, at ~10K steps daily.</strong></h3>
<p>Since 1 Jan 2018, I've steadily increased my walking average until Aug 2018. Then my legs started aching. So I cut it down until Jan 2019. In Feb, I resumed and was fairly steady until May 2020. To complement workouts like this, products that are aimed for <strong><a href="https://theislandnow.com/blog-112/best-testosterone-booster/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">men over 50</a></strong> can be used.</p>
<p>In May, my wife refused to let me walk for more than an hour a day. It took me a few months to convince her and level up. I ended 2020 averaging a little over <a href="/blog/walking-10000-steps-a-day/">10K steps for the year</a>.</p>

![](/blog/assets/image-png-9.webp)

<h3><strong>I'm becoming more regular. I walked 10K/day 15% more in 2020 than in 2018.</strong></h3>
<p>2018: I walked 10K steps almost half the time.<br/>2019: it grew to a bit more, to 56%.<br/>2020: I walked 10K steps a day almost two-thirds of the time.</p>

![](/blog/assets/image-png-10.webp)

<h3><strong>But in May 2020, I went for 5 days without walking even 3K steps.</strong></h3>
<p>In 2018, I started being more and more regular until my leg started aching.<br/>2019 was fairly consistent.<br/>2020 is when I applied brakes again -- for very different reasons.</p>

![](/blog/assets/image-png-11.webp)

<p>I've never gone for 5 days without walking even 3K/day before, since 2018. At most, it was 3 days at a stretch.</p>
<p><strong>But when my wife refused to let me walk for more than an hour a day in May 2020, I went on strike! ðŸ˜‰</strong></p>
<h3><strong>I walk ~77 min daily. This has increased over the years.</strong></h3>
<p>In 2020, this has gone up slightly to 84 min -- but it's still under an hour-and-half. I spend most of this time on calls or listening to audio books / podcasts.<br/>Instead of spending it with my family.</p>

![](/blog/assets/image-png-12.webp)

<p><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/naveengattu/">Naveen</a> is usually to blame. But this happens rarely. I walked 20K steps just 6 times over the last 3 years.</p>

<h3><strong>I want to walk faster. I walk at ~4.4 km/hr. My target is 5 km/hr.</strong></h3>
<p>Walking at over 5 km/hr speeds the heart up and improves metabolism. (Or so I've heard.)</p>
<p>I was steadily going towards 5 km/hr in my early days of walking. I slowed down starting Aug 2018, since my legs were aching. Then I picked up speed in end-2018.</p>
<p>I slowed down again in Nov 2019 -- and I don't remember why.</p>
<p>In Jun 2020, I started walking much faster -- mainly to complete 10K steps within the hour my wife gave me. That seems to have had a lasting impact. I walked faster overall in 2020.</p>

![](/blog/assets/image-png-14.webp)

<h3><strong>I've managed fast walking 66 times in 2020, a bit more than before.</strong></h3>
<p>In Jun 2020, I walked at over 5 km / hr on 20 / 30 days -- a very consistent high speed. I've never gotten close to this any other month.<br/>(Clearly, there are adverse effects of being able to convince my wife.)</p>

![](/blog/assets/image-15.webp)

<h3><strong>The fastest I walked was in 2018, at 6.8 km/hr. It might have led to my leg aches.</strong></h3>
<p>My top 5 walking speeds were in 2018. In 2020, I've managed to walk faster than 6 km / hr just once.</p>

<p><a href="https://www.omnicalculator.com/sports/stride-length#average-stride-length">The normal stride/height ratio is 0.43</a>. I'm 5'8". My stride is 2.4 ft. That's almost exactly 0.43 times my height. So all is well.</p>
<h3><strong>By increasing my stride by 2 inches, I can cover 10,000 steps in 8 min less time.</strong></h3>
<p>For every inch I lengthen my stride, I walk ~0.2km/hr faster.</p>
<p>I've walked with a stride as long as 32", which is 3" more than my 2020 average stride. <strong>By walking with a 2" longer stride, I can be 9.2% faster.</strong></p>

![](/blog/assets/image-17.webp)

<h3><strong>So in 2021, I plan to get healthier (and scolded less) with a 2" longer stride.</strong></h3>
<p>A longer stride means a faster walk. That's a good cardio exercise.<br/>A faster walk also means that it takes less time. So I'll get beaten up less.<br/>All it takes is stretching my legs 2" more. Might hurt a bit. I'll report on this when I know better.</p>
